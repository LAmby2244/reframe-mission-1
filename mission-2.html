Mission 02 · Chapter 4

The Immunity Map

You are not failing to change. You are succeeding at something else — something hidden. This mission maps what you are protecting and why.

Where you're coming from

In Mission 1 you built the honest case for change. You named what the last six months have really looked like, identified something meaningful to work on, and wrote a commitment. You arrived at something real.

This mission takes that commitment and asks a harder question: if you know what needs to change, and you want to change it, why hasn't it happened yet? The answer is not weakness or lack of willpower. There is something else running — an intelligent protection system doing exactly what it was built to do. This mission finds it.

Part 1
Experiment — Uncover Your Internal Resistance

We resist change not because we don't want to grow, but because we are unconsciously protecting ourselves from something we fear. Robert Kegan's Immunity to Change model shows that we each carry hidden commitments that run directly against the change we say we want. Work through the four steps below with the shift you identified in Mission 1.

1
Validate your commitment

Before you go deeper, check that the shift you've named meets these criteria:

It is genuinely important to you — a meaningful shift if you could improve dramatically
Others in your life would recognise it — they'd agree it matters
It is yours — something within your control, not about changing others
It is a repeated challenge — something you've heard before or noticed over time

Reflect — The Cost and the Possibility

Now that you can see the hidden system — the competing commitment, the big assumption — look at what it has been costing you. And look at what becomes possible if you start to loosen its grip.

Be specific. Name the person, the relationship, the opportunity that slowly disappears.

Share — Test It With Someone You Trust

Share what you've uncovered with someone you trust. The hidden competing commitments and big assumptions are often easier to see from the outside. Choose someone who will be honest with you, not just comfortable.
