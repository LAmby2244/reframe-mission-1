Part 3
Share — Bring It Into the Room

Once you've named your reality, take it somewhere. Talk through your reflections with someone you trust — a friend, a colleague, a coach. Hearing yourself say it out loud to another person anchors it in a way that writing alone cannot.

◈
Why this matters
Sharing creates accountability. It also creates new insight — the person who hears you will often reflect something back that shifts how you see it. You don't have to share everything. Share what feels most alive, or most stuck.

Part 4
Practice — Write Both Versions

Finally, now that you've reflected on what's working, what's not, and what's keeping you stuck, it's time to name the first draft of your shift. You'll write two versions of your truth — one that reflects your current reality (the tension), and another that declares your new identity (the shift).

◈
How this works
Version 1 acknowledges both what you love and what is in the way — it makes your case for change honest and specific. Version 2 shifts from fixing a problem to stepping into an identity. When you see yourself this way, your habits and choices naturally start to align.
Version 1 — The BUT statement: what I love, and what's in the way.

e.g. "I love the impact I make in my role, but I'm constantly overwhelmed because I take on too much."
