Create your story lifeline

Zoom out from the present and reflect on your life story. Consider the defining moments that have made you who you are — both the highs and the lows: milestones, setbacks, transformative relationships, unexpected detours.

  • Choose a starting point — early childhood, a pivotal moment, wherever feels right
  • Mark significant highs (above the line) and lows (below the line)
  • The higher or lower you place a note, the more intense the moment
  • Don't overthink the details — plot what stands out intuitively
  • Click the pencil icon on any note to edit it
Your lifeline works best on a larger screen — come back on a laptop or desktop to map your moments on the interactive timeline.

Be seen

Find someone you trust — a thoughtful friend, a coach, a mentor — and share your lifeline. The act of being seen and heard around your own story can offer clarity you cannot find alone.

Share with them:
  • What this exercise brought up for you
  • The patterns you noticed
  • The moment or chapter that carries the most unresolved charge
Ask them:
  • What do you see in my story that I might be missing?
  • What assumptions might I be making about what these events mean?
  • What do you think I'm ready to let go of?
