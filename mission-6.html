Mission 06 · Chapter 9

Taming Your Triggers

A trigger is not a weakness. It is the moment your body decides the past is happening again. This mission builds your map of those moments — so you can meet them differently.

Where you're coming from

In Mission 5 you named your values — traced where they came from, mapped the spectrum of how you hold them, and began to see the limiting beliefs running underneath them. You now have a clearer picture of what matters most to you.

This mission takes that map into the live territory of your daily experience. A trigger is not a weakness — it is a signpost for learning. It is the moment your body decides the past is happening again, usually because one of your values or beliefs is being challenged. Understanding your triggers is how you start to meet them differently, rather than being run by them.

Part 4
Practice — Press Pause

Press Pause is a three-step practice for the moment you notice a trigger starting. You can't always change the situation. But you can notice the trigger and own your response.

Step 1
Notice the trigger
You cannot always change the situation — but you can notice the trigger and own your response.
Ask: What is my trigger right now?
Step 2
Acknowledge the emotion
Emotions are data, not threats. Name what you are feeling without letting it run you.
Ask: What am I feeling? What am I believing to be true?
Step 3
Create space
The space between trigger and response is where your choice lives. Pause before you act.
Ask: What will help me right now?
Which specific trigger will I work on first?
